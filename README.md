# Hospital-Dataset-Dashboard
The dashboard was created using Excel, with data cleaned using Power Query, after which pivot tables and visualizations were developed to present a structured view of hospital performance across key metrics.

The dashboard reflects a total of 15 patients, providing a compact but insightful view of hospital activity across demographics, admissions, and outcomes. Gender distribution is nearly balanced, with 8 males (53%) and 7 females (47%), indicating no significant gender bias in patient intake. The patient population spans multiple age groups, with a stronger presence within the working age bracket, suggesting the hospital primarily serves an economically active segment.

Admission patterns show that 4 patients (27%) were emergency cases, while 11 patients (73%) were non-emergency or planned admissions. This highlights that although scheduled care dominates, emergency cases still form a meaningful portion of total inflow and require consistent operational readiness.

The average length of stay stands at 5.6 days, indicating moderate treatment durations. However, this average likely reflects a mix of short and extended stays, with a smaller group of patients requiring longer hospitalization and consuming a higher share of resources. These cases are typically associated with more complex conditions.

Readmission trends reveal that 4 patients (27%) were readmitted, while 11 patients (73%) were not. This level of readmission is notable relative to the dataset size and suggests potential gaps in discharge processes or follow-up care. It raises a key question around whether specific diagnoses or patient groups are driving repeat visits.

Outcome analysis shows strong survival rates, with 13 patients (87%) surviving and 2 patients (13%) deceased. While outcomes are largely positive, the mortality cases, when considered alongside emergency admissions and longer stays, point to possible late-stage presentations or critical conditions at admission.

The dashboard highlights clear operational pressure points:

Emergency admissions at 27% require efficient triage and response systems
Readmissions at 27% suggest the need for stronger post-treatment monitoring
An average stay of 5.6 days indicates resource concentration in more complex cases

Focusing on these areas will improve both patient outcomes and overall hospital efficiency.
